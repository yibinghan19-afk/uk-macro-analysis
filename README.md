# UK Macroeconomic Analysis (2000–2024)

## Overview
An exploratory analysis of three key UK macroeconomic indicators — GDP growth, CPI inflation, and unemployment — from 2000 to 2024, 
examining how major economic shocks affected the UK economy and whether traditional macroeconomic theories held during these periods.

## Key Questions
- How did the 2008 Financial Crisis affect GDP, inflation, and unemployment?
- Did the 2022 inflation surge follow the Phillips Curve relationship?
- How did different types of economic shocks produce different outcomes?

## Key Findings
1. The 2008 crisis provided a near-textbook validation of Okun's Law, but the recovery was notably slow — unemployment took 7 years to 
return to pre-crisis levels, suggesting structural factors beyond what the theory captures.

2. The Phillips Curve was effectively flat during 2000–2019, but the 2020–2024 period saw an upward shift driven by supply-side shocks 
(post-COVID disruptions and the 2022 energy price spike) — not a movement along the curve.
